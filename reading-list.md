# Reading List

The core texts I'm working through during my year of quant preparation. Listed roughly in order of priority and depth of study.

## Tier A — Core reading (every chapter, with exercises)

1. **Shreve, *Stochastic Calculus for Finance II: Continuous-Time Models*** (Springer, 2004)
   The canonical reference for the math underlying derivatives pricing. Working through Ch 1–11 across M1–M5.

2. **Cartea, Jaimungal, Penalva, *Algorithmic and High-Frequency Trading*** (Cambridge, 2015)
   Market microstructure and stochastic optimal control for trading. Foundation for the Avellaneda–Stoikov-style market-making project.

3. **James, Witten, Hastie, Tibshirani, Taylor, *An Introduction to Statistical Learning with Python*** (Springer, 2023)
   Statistical learning fundamentals with code labs. Working through chapters with implementations in [islp-labs](https://github.com/<your-username>/islp-labs).

## Tier A — Interview preparation

4. **Crack, *Heard on the Street: Quantitative Questions from Wall Street Job Interviews*** (24th ed.)
   Year-long sequenced practice, ~3 problems per day across Q1 (Quant & Logic), Q2 (Derivatives), Q3 (Other Financial Economics), Q4 (Statistics), Q5 (Non-Quantitative).

## Tier B — Supplementary

5. **Hull, *Options, Futures, and Other Derivatives*** — practitioner-flavoured complement to Shreve
6. **Joshi, *The Concepts and Practice of Mathematical Finance*** — bridges intuition and rigour
7. **Wilmott, *Paul Wilmott on Quantitative Finance*** — reference for breadth

## Tier C — Thesis references

8. Pham, *Continuous-time Stochastic Control and Optimization with Financial Applications*
9. Fleming, Soner, *Controlled Markov Processes and Viscosity Solutions*
10. Yong, Zhou, *Stochastic Controls: Hamiltonian Systems and HJB Equations*
11. Bardi, Capuzzo-Dolcetta, *Optimal Control and Viscosity Solutions of Hamilton–Jacobi–Bellman Equations*
12. Kushner, Dupuis, *Numerical Methods for Stochastic Control Problems in Continuous Time*
